About Me

I’m Lei Zhang. I work on machine learning frameworks and compilers within Google.

Currently my main focus is to push forward leveraging a compilation based approach and Vulkan compute for ML inference on GPUs. I maintain the SPIR-V dialect and conversions in MLIR and contribute to IREE, a MLIR-based end-to-end compiler and runtime that draws lots of design principles for Vulkan.

Previously I contributed to various general Vulkan and SPIR-V toolchain projects, including but not limited to DirectXShaderCompiler (for its HLSL to SPIR-V compilation), Shaderc (a collection of tools, libraries, and tests for Vulkan shader compilation), SPIRV-Tools (SPIR-V module assembler, disassembler, optimizer, etc.), SPIRV-Headers (SPIR-V machine-readable definitions), Glslang (for its GLSL to SPIR-V compilation), VK-GL-CTS (Vulkan/OpenGL conformance test suite). I also created and open sourced a few SPIR-V projects in the Rust programming language: rspirv (Rust implementation of SPIR-V module processing functionalities) and shaderc-rs (Rust bindings for the shaderc library).
